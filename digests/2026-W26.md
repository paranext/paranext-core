## Proposed process improvements (human-gated)

**Diagnosis - weakest step is the PR hand-off, not find/refute/repro.** The find→refute→repro
pipeline is healthy: survivors are real and shipped, and the 19 suppressed findings were
correctly rejected (mostly out-of-repo or non-reproducible). The bottleneck is *downstream*
of the loop: verified draft PRs accumulate unreviewed (7 open, ages 1-9 days; lifetime 1 of
9 merged; the one closed-unmerged was never reviewed). The single existing safety valve
(closed-unmerged rate >= 34%) is **blind to this failure mode** - a pile of open, unlooked-at
PRs never trips it. The loop can therefore silently degrade into a stockpile of un-acted-upon
PRs - the inverse of the churn it was built to fight. The three proposals below target that
gap, in priority order. (Not applied - for team review.)

1. **Add an open-PR-pressure safety valve (the missing symmetric guard).** Today's valve in
   `lib/aggregate-week.mjs` `shouldPause()` only considers `closedUnmergedRate`. Add config
   thresholds `safetyValve.maxOpenPrs` (e.g. 5) and `safetyValve.openPrAgeDaysToPause`
   (e.g. 7), have `computeWeeklyMetrics` emit `openPrs` + `oldestOpenPrAgeDays` (derive age
   from a `createdAt` lookback in `ledger.mjs refresh-prs`), and trip the pause when the open
   queue is both deep and stale. The daily loop already halts new PRs on `state.paused`, so
   the wiring is minimal. *Rationale: makes the loop unable to quietly grow an unreviewed
   backlog; forces a human to drain before more is produced.*

2. **Route each draft PR to a human instead of leaving it invisible.** Every open PR is
   `REVIEW_REQUIRED` with no assigned reviewer, and #2445 was closed while still an unreviewed
   draft - drafts with no reviewer don't surface in anyone's queue. Without marking PRs ready
   (still forbidden), add to `/quality-sweep` step 8: `gh pr edit <n> --add-reviewer <code-steward>`
   on open, and have the weekly digest @-mention the steward with the open-PR list as a
   standing nudge. *Rationale: the trust metric (merge rate) is unmeasurable while PRs sit
   unlooked-at; a verified fix nobody sees prevents zero defects.*

3. **Pre-filter the Jira seed pool to in-repo-fixable issues.** ~10 of 19 suppressions this
   week were "root cause in external `platform-editor`/scripture-editors or another extension."
   That find/refute budget is spent on tickets the loop structurally cannot ship. Tighten the
   step-1 JQL (or add a post-query filter) to exclude issues whose component/label marks them
   external-editor-owned, and de-prioritize those needing live DBL/S&R credentials that can't
   be reproduced headlessly. *Rationale: raises the effective survivor-to-PR yield by spending
   the daily find budget on actionable tickets.*

_Minor (metric honesty, not a process change):_ compute `prMergeRate` over **resolved** PRs
only and report a separate `prsPending` count, so an unresolved-but-healthy week (like this
one: 4 PRs, all young) does not read as 0% trust. Edit `computeWeeklyMetrics` + `renderDigest`.
